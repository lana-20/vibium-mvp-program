# Vibium Contribution Framework

**Establishing the registry of human-agent collaboration.**

Vibium is a community-driven initiative built on the principle that while creativity should be free and unburdened, professional responsibility must be fairly compensated. This document outlines the clear boundaries between community-led volunteer contributions and core operational work.

## 1. The Volunteer Sphere (Ambassadors & MVPs)

The volunteer sphere is the engine of community growth and ecosystem expansion.

### Nature of Work:
- **Self-Directed:** Choose projects based on personal interest and technical curiosity.  
- **Flexible:** No required hours, fixed deadlines, or minimum coverage expectations.  
- **Impact-Driven:** Focused on experimentation, education, and brand advocacy.  

### Core Activities:
- **Educational Content:** Authoring tutorials, videos, and technical explainers.  
- **Peer Mentorship:** Ad-hoc assistance within the Discord community.  
- **Ecosystem Growth:** Developing community plugins or improving documentation clarity.  

---

## 2. The Compensated Sphere (Bounties & Contracts)

This sphere covers high-accountability tasks critical to the stability and growth of Vibium infrastructure.

### Nature of Work:
- **Defined Scope:** Specific technical requirements and fixed delivery dates.  
- **Accountability:** Work is subject to formal performance standards and peer review.  
- **Business Critical:** Tasks affecting system uptime, security, or enterprise utility.  

### Core Activities:
- **Protocol Engineering:** Security audits, vulnerability fixes, and core SDK maintenance.  
- **Technical Assets:** Enterprise benchmarking and official documentation architecture.  
- **Representation:** Official workshops, training, and partner consulting.  

---

## 3. The Boundary Rules

A task must transition to the compensated sphere if it meets any of the following:

- **High Accountability:** Failure to deliver risks system outages or security vulnerabilities.  
- **Scheduled Responsibility:** Requires "on duty" status or regular moderation shifts.  
- **Operational Overhead:** Involves internal maintenance or administrative coordination.  
- **Proprietary Scope:** Output is intended for internal use rather than open-source release.  

### The "Danger Zone" Test

Before accepting or assigning work, apply this binary:

> **"If this task stops tomorrow, does a critical system or process break?"**

- **No:** Volunteer contribution.  
- **Yes:** Compensated responsibility.  

---

## 4. The Ascension Path

Vibium prioritizes internal community members for paid opportunities.

- **Contribute:** Build a track record as an Ambassador or MVP.  
- **Demonstrate:** Show technical consistency and cultural alignment.  
- **Ascend:** Receive invitations for bounties, fixed-term contracts, or core roles.  

---

## 5. Institutional Commitments

- No unpaid operational or administrative roles.  
- No "soft expectations" of free labor for business-critical tasks.  
- No replacement of monetary compensation with merchandise for core work.  
- No requirement for Ambassadors to serve as technical support staff.  

> **We will never ask our community to do work that we would not pay for internally.**
